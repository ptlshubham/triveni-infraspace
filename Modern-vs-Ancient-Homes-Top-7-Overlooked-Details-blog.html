Modernization has wrapped its tentacles around the whole world. Everything is changing to keep up with the latest trends, including technology, fashion, and home appliances. Traditional homes, like those in the Colonial and Victorian styles that were popular in the 19th and 20th centuries, are slowly coming back.

Modern vs. Ancient Homes: Top 7 Overlooked Details

Ancient architecture may have been a source of inspiration for modern architecture that makes its own statement and has a clean design. But they are not just the result of going back and fixing up an old way of building. Modern houses are the result of careful planning, design, and construction. They also reflect their owners' personalities in a sensible way.

So, what are the biggest differences between houses built in the past and houses built today? How does using methods like Vastu Shastra affect the way smart homes are built? Let's try to find answers to these questions today!

1. Easy is better than hard

Ancient architecture from the 19th and 20th centuries was based on elegant and spacious designs. Modern houses, on the other hand, are built to be as useful as possible while keeping costs down. This principle is true even for the smallest parts of how it is done.

Also, I want to talk about the size and layout. In traditional, old architecture, many single units are put together to make a huge building.

People say that old architecture likes to cover up its features with too many details and decorations. On the other hand, modern architecture focuses on making a structure that shows off its sharp edges. "Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication," said Da Vinci.

2. Changes in the way it's done

Ancient buildings had beautiful handwork that was done by local craftsmen. Modern architecture, on the other hand, depends on skill and style.

As building technology improved quickly, modern architecture and building started to use basic principles like good planning, scheduling, and management.

This has helped a lot with optimising the project, checking the quality of the materials, and making sure the design is right, which has led to a well-built building form.

3. Differences in elements

From the furniture to the curtains, there are many noticeable differences between modern and old homes. Modern homes have to pay a lot of attention to storage.

Traditional homes put a lot of emphasis on ornamental pieces, elaborate interiors, and artwork, but modern homes care more about simple designs that make the most money.

Take the example of small kitchen counters in modern homes that are both stylish and have more space for storage. And floors that are lighter in colour to let in more light, which brightens the space and makes it look bigger.

4. The shape and figure

Lack of land is a big problem in the modern world. Back then, there was plenty of space for buildings to grow. Now, vertical construction is growing faster than horizontal construction. And building methods like MIVAN, RCC, and even 3D printing have a big impact on the shapes.

Modern architecture is more willing to try new things and break away from what has always been done.

Investing is a key part of any field, and architecture is no different. Rapid changes in the modern world have given people more choices. Back then, only big buildings were built with a lot of money. Now, things are changing, and even commercial buildings around the world are getting more work and money.

5. Material alternatives

In ancient architecture, there weren't many materials to choose from, but today there are a lot! For example, ancient Terra Cota Bricks have been easily replaced by concrete blocks, and plastering has been made easier by using gypsum boards.

Not only have the materials changed a lot, but so has the way they are put together. For example, traditional rammed earth has been replaced by hydraulically compacted rammed earth, which is much more efficient.

Also, in the new age, the industry has become very specific, and professionals know exactly what they need instead of making do with the few options they have. Alvar Aalto, a famous Finnish architect, said, "Modern architecture does not mean using immature new materials; the main thing is to refine materials in a more human direction."

6. Changing the way convenience works

Unlike old homes, smart homes of today use technology to make things better and make sure convenience is at its best.

Home automation has changed the way things are done by giving people the power to control things like light and temperature through the internet.

A smart home that is automated can also have features like control over the air quality, voice control, and better security.

7. The vastu shastra system

Vastu Shastra is an old, traditional system that covers all aspects of building design, including space geometry, arrangement, orientation, layout, measurement, and scaling

Back then, projects were done by following the rules of this system verbally. Now, Vastu Shastra as a whole is studied as a course and is religiously used in architecture.

Modern architecture is designed and built based on the basic rules of Vastu, such as where different parts and rooms are put in a building.

Even though ancient architecture and modern architecture have very different ideas about beauty and how to design, they do have some things in common. Say, in the case of bringing harmony to proportions and scale co-ordinations in architecture. When we look at both times, the materials that were available also affected how things looked.

Modern architecture has a lot of uses and can work in many different areas, such as multi-utilitarian designs, modern energy, designs that save money, and architecture that is friendly to the environment and cares about it.

Also, some parts of ancient architecture have been taken out of modern architecture on purpose because they no longer serve a purpose. Still, many changes have been made to architecture in recent years to meet the needs of the time. One example is a building that can be used for both business and housing.
