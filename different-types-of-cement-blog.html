Looking at the various types of cement on the market can make your head spin as you try to figure out which one is best suited for your construction.

7 Different Types of Cement in India

Cement is essential in today's architectural world because it is used for mortar, plaster, concrete, and so on. Its toughness is generally classified by grade. These grades are the fundamental indicators of compression strength. Which is measured in Mega-Pascal (Mpa) after 28 days of setting. They are also given Indian Standard codes to make it easier for Civil Engineers to install.

To save you time and effort, we have categorized the various types of cement used in India today and their applicability.

Starting with Ordinary Portland Cement, which, in comparison to other types of cement, is THE MOST USED CEMENT IN THE WORLD. It's made from raw argillaceous, calcareous, and gypsum materials. According to Indian Standards, there are three grades of cement: OPC 33 Grade, OPC 43 Grade, and OPC 53 Grade cement.

1. Ordinary Portland Cement 33 Grade

It is used up to M20 grade concrete for general civil construction purposes such as plastering, flooring, and masonry. This cement has low compressive strength and low heat hydration, resulting in less cracking. Yet, this type of cement is less common in the market. It has the Indian Standard code 269.

2. Ordinary Portland Cement 43 Grade

They are used for general civil purposes such as plastering and flooring up to M 30 concrete grades, like OPC 33-grade cement. At 28 days, this cement has the lowest compression strength of 43 Mega-Pascal. Among the various types of cement available, this is still available in the market due to its extensive use in the construction industry. Despite the fact that it is being replaced by the blended form of cement. The Indian Standard code for Ordinary Portland Cement is 8112.

3. Ordinary Portland Cement 53 Grade

This type of cement is used with a higher concrete grade than M30 for general purposes as well as for the construction of bridges, roads, and so on. At 28 days of curing, they achieve the lowest compression strength of 53 Mega-Pascal. They are also used in reinforced cement concrete, to achieve higher strength and ductility. Application has also been observed in cement grouts and instant plugging mortars. It has the Indian Standard code 12269.

4. Portland Slag Cement (PSC)

Portland Slag cement is preferred over Ordinary Portland Cement as it does not crack when exposed to harsh environments. Such as wastewater treatment or marine applications. Because of its high strength, it is best suited for high-rise structures or long-term services such as roads or bridges. Its properties work in tandem with Portland cement to increase strength, lowering permeability, and improve resistance to various chemical reactions. This, unlike other types of cement, is only available in certain markets. The Indian Standard code for this cement is 455.

5. Colored Cement: White

Even though Colored Portland Cement is more expensive than Ordinary Portland Cement, it does have its uses and benefits. Made from raw materials like iron oxide and manganese oxide. Used to improve the look of buildings inside and out, for things like terrazzo tiles, decorative concrete products like idols, flooring, and special effects that aren't structural. As its name suggests, this cement is white, and its chemical makeup and physical properties also meet the requirements for Ordinary Portland cement. The Indian Standard code for the coloured cement is 8042.

6. Portland Pozzolana Cement (PPC)

Portland Pozzolana is known for being fine, having a high level of impermeability, and being a good corrosion resistor. This makes the concrete dense, which makes the structure last longer. Because it is very waterproof and stays strong for more than 90 days, this type of cement is used instead of regular Portland cement in places with bad soil. So, it is used to build marine structures, mass concrete structures like bridge footings in harsh conditions, hydraulic structures like dams and retaining walls, and even basic masonry mortar and plastering works. You can also buy Portland Pozzolana Cement in stores. The Indian Standard code for this cement is 1489 P-2.

7.Hydrophobic Portland Cement

Hydrophobic Portland cement isn't easy to find because there are so many different kinds of cement on the market. It's also more expensive than ordinary Portland cement, but it's best for use in harsh, cold weather because it's made to meet the needs of areas with a lot of rain to make it last longer. As the name suggests, it is made with a chemical coating that makes it good at keeping water away. It is also not affected by high humidity, so it can be stored for a long time. This cement is used to build spillways, dams, and other underwater structures. The Indian Standard code for this cement is 8043.
